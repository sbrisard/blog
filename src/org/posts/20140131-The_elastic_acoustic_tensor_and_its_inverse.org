#+TITLE: The elastic acoustic tensor and its inverse
#+DATE: <2014-01-31 Fri>

#+BEGIN_ABSTRACT
In this post, I will introduce the acoustic tensor of linearly elastic materials. Closed-form expressions of the inverse of this tensor can be derived in the case of /isotropic/ materials. This will later come in handy to derive closed-form expressions of the periodic Green operator for strains.
#+END_ABSTRACT

We consider a linearly elastic material with stiffness $\stiffness$. For any wave-vector $\vec k$, the elastic acoustic tensor $\tens A(\vec k)$ is defined as follows

\begin{equation}
\label{eq:1}
\tens A(\vec k)=\vec k\cdot\stiffness\cdot\vec k=k^2\vec n\cdot\stiffness\cdot\vec n,
\end{equation}

where $k$ is the amplitude of the wave-vector $\vec k$, $k=\sqrt{\vec k\cdot\vec k}$, and $\vec n$ is its direction ($\vec k=k\vec n$). The acoustic tensor is used to assess material stability (Bigoni and Zaccaria, /European Journal of Mechanics- A/Solids/ *13(5)*, pp. 621â€“638, 1994) and study the propagation of waves ([[https://doi.org/10.1016/j.ijengsci.2013.07.006][Gentile and Straughan, 2013]]). As far as we are concerned, the derivation of the periodic Green operator for strains will require the expressions of the inverse of the acoustic tensor@@html:<!-- TODO Insert hyperlink -->@@. This is the topic of the present post@@html:<!-- more -->@@, which is restricted to isotropic materials: the stiffness tensor $\stiffness$ of isotropic materials is therefore a linear combination of the [[file:./20140112-Elastic_constants_of_an_isotropic_material-03.org][isotropic projection tensors]] $\sphprojiv,\devprojiv$

\begin{equation}
\label{eq:2}
\stiffness=d\kappa\sphprojiv+2\shearmod\devprojiv
\end{equation}

To compute the elastic acoustic tensor, we therefore need to find the expressions of $\vec n\cdot\sphprojiv\cdot\vec n$ and $\vec n\cdot\devprojiv\cdot\vec n$, for any unit vector $\vec n$. We start with the computation of $\vec n\cdot\identiv\cdot\vec n$, where $\identiv$ denotes the fourth-rank identity tensor. We have

\begin{equation*}
\left(\vec n\cdot\identiv\cdot\vec n\right)_{jk}=n_iI_{ijkl}n_l=n_in_l\frac12\left(\delta_{il}\delta_{jk}+\delta_{jl}\delta_{ik}\right)=\frac12\left(n_in_i\delta_{jk}+n_jn_k\right).
\end{equation*}

Since $\vec n$ is a unit vector, we have $n_in_i=1$, and

\begin{equation*}
\vec n\cdot\identiv\cdot\vec n=\frac12\left(\identii+\vec n\otimes\vec n\right),
\end{equation*}

where $\identii$ denotes the second-rank identity tensor. It will be convenient to introduce the projectors $\tensii p$ and $\tensii q$, defined as follows

\begin{equation*}
\tensii p=\vec n\otimes\vec n\qquad\tensii q=\identii-\tensii p,
\end{equation*}

or, using indices

\begin{equation*}
p_{ij}=n_in_j\qquad q_{ij}=\delta_{ij}-n_in_j.
\end{equation*}

It can readily be verified that
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:3}
\tensii p\cdot\tensii p=\tensii p,\qquad\tensii q\cdot\tensii q=\tensii q,\qquad\tensii p\cdot\tensii q=\tensii q\cdot\tensii p=\tensii0,
\end{equation}

and

\begin{equation}
\label{eq:4}
\vec n\cdot\identiv\cdot\vec n=\tensii p+\frac12\tensii q.
\end{equation}

Similarly

\begin{equation}
\label{eq:5}
\vec n\cdot\sphprojiv\cdot\vec n=\frac1d\vec n\cdot\left(\identii\otimes\identii\right)\cdot\vec n=\frac1d\vec n\otimes\vec n=\frac1d\tensii p.
\end{equation}

Finally, combining identity $\devprojiv=\identiv-\sphprojiv$ with Eqs. \eqref{eq:4} and \eqref{eq:5}

\begin{equation}
\label{eq:6}
\vec n\cdot\devprojiv\cdot\vec n=\frac{d-1}d\tensii p+\frac12\tensii q.
\end{equation}

The acoustic tensor of an elastic, linear, isotropic material is obtained from Eqs. \eqref{eq:1}, \eqref{eq:2}, \eqref{eq:5} and \eqref{eq:6}

\begin{equation*}
\tens A(\vec n)=\left(\kappa+2\shearmod\frac{d-1}d\right)\tensii p+\shearmod\tensii q.
\end{equation*}

From [[file:./20140112-Elastic_constants_of_an_isotropic_material-03.org][this post]], it can readily be verified that

\begin{equation*}
\kappa+2\shearmod\frac{d-1}d=2\shearmod\frac{1-\nu}{1-2\nu},
\end{equation*}

this identity being true in both 3D and plane strain elasticity. The acoustic tensor therefore reads

\begin{equation*}
\tens A(\vec k)=k^2\shearmod\left(\frac{2\left(1-\nu\right)}{1-2\nu}\tensii p+\tensii q\right).
\end{equation*}

Finally, using the properties of the projectors $\tensii p$ and $\tensii q$ [see Eq. \eqref{eq:3}], the /inverse/ of the acoustic tensor can be derived

\begin{equation*}
\tens A^{-1}(\vec k)=\frac1{k^2\shearmod}\left(\frac{1-2\nu}{2\left(1-\nu\right)}\tensii p+\tensii q\right).
\end{equation*}
